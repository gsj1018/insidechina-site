China to USA Shipping: All Options Compared

Epacket vs. SAL vs. DHL vs. sea freight -- I break down every method so you can pick the right one and protect your margins.

Why Shipping is the Make-or-Break Cost

I have seen dozens of dropshipping businesses fail not because the product was bad, not because the marketing was wrong, but because the founder did not correctly calculate shipping costs and discovered the real margin was negative after launching.

Always calculate landed cost before you launch.

All Shipping Methods Compared

Epacket (ePacket)

Cost: $3.50-$8.00 per parcel (under 2kg) | Speed: 10-20 business days | Risk: Low
Best for: Testing products, small orders, AliExpress dropshipping.

China Post Registered Air Mail

Cost: $2.50-$5.00 per parcel (under 2kg) | Speed: 15-30 business days | Risk: Medium
Best for: Ultra-budget shipping, non-urgent products.

SAL (Surface Air Lifted)

Cost: $4.00-$9.00 per parcel | Speed: 15-25 business days | Risk: Low-Medium
Best for: Balance between cost and speed for parcels over 2kg.

DHL / FedEx / UPS Express

Cost: $25-$80+ per parcel | Speed: 3-7 business days | Risk: Very low
Best for: Urgent orders, high-value products, bulk samples.

Sea Freight (Full Container / LCL)

Cost: $0.50-$2.50 per kg (LCL) | Speed: 20-40 days transit + customs | Risk: Low
Best for: Bulk orders over 100kg+, establishing inventory.

How to Choose the Right Method

Scenario Best Method Why
Testing a new productEpacket / AliExpress shippingFast enough for testing, included in AliExpress price
Regular dropshipping (under 2kg)Epacket or SALBest cost/speed balance for recurring small orders
Bulk inventory (200kg+)Sea freight (LCL or FCL)Economical for large volumes, takes time but worth it
High-value urgent sampleDHL / FedEx ExpressFast, reliable, includes customs clearance

How to Reduce Shipping Costs

  1. Negotiate with your supplier -- ask if they have preferred freight forwarders. Suppliers ship from China daily and have negotiated rates you can benefit from.
  2. Use freight forwarders -- companies like CURCHOD, Flexport, and small forwarders consolidate your goods with others to reduce per-unit costs.
  3. Optimize packaging weight -- lighter packaging = lower dimensional weight charges.
  4. Ship to a US warehouse -- if selling on Amazon FBA or using a 3PL, ship bulk sea freight to the US warehouse first.
  5. Use dropshipping services -- some 1688 suppliers offer one piece dropshipping where they handle all shipping logistics.

Shipping Mistakes That Kill Margins

  1. Forgetting customs duties -- USA de minimis threshold is $800. Below that, you generally avoid duties. Above it, factor in 6-10% on most goods.
  2. Ignoring dimensional weight -- a large but light product gets charged by its volumetric size, not actual weight.
  3. Not insuring high-value shipments -- for products over $100 in value, insurance is worth the 1-2% extra cost.
  4. Using express for everything -- $60 DHL when $6 epacket would have arrived in 2 weeks is a margin killer.

The Landed Cost Formula

Landed Cost = Product Cost + Chinese Domestic Shipping + International Freight + Customs Duties + US Last-Mile Delivery

Only after calculating landed cost can you know your true margin. Never launch a product without this number.
