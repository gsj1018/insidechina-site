The Complete 1688 Guide for Western Dropshippers (2026)

1688 is where Chinese sellers actually buy from -- prices are 30-70% lower than Alibaba. Here is how to use it without speaking a word of Chinese.

GS
gsj1018ChinaSource

What is 1688 and Why Should You Care?

Let me start with a number: the same product that costs $5 on Alibaba costs $2.50 on 1688. Sometimes the gap is even bigger. 1688 is a B2B wholesale platform owned by Alibaba Group -- designed for the Chinese domestic market, not international buyers. That is why most Western entrepreneurs never find it.

1688 serves Chinese resellers, small retailers, and e-commerce sellers who buy in bulk directly from manufacturers. Since these buyers speak Chinese, understand supplier culture, and do not need international payment methods, the platform was never built for foreigners. But it is absolutely accessible to Westerners -- you just need the right tools and approach.

1688 vs Alibaba -- The Real Difference

You might wonder: if both are Alibaba Group platforms, why is 1688 so much cheaper? Here is the honest answer:

  • Alibaba.com is optimized for international buyers -- English, USD pricing, trade assurance, longer lead times for export
  • 1688.com is for Chinese domestic buyers -- Chinese interface, CNY pricing, faster fulfillment, smaller MOQs
  • Suppliers on 1688 are often the same factories as Alibaba, but they charge a premium on Alibaba to cover marketing, translation, and export handling
When to use each:
Use Alibaba when you want Trade Assurance, English support, and are ordering 500+ units. Use 1688 when you want the lowest possible price, are ordering 10-500 units, and can handle basic Chinese supplier communication.

How to Set Up 1688 (Even as a Foreigner)

1688 does not accept international credit cards directly. There are two main ways to solve this:

Option A: Use a Buying Agent (Recommended for Beginners)

Services like Pandabuuy, Wegobuy, and Baoproduce act as your proxy. You browse 1688, add items to their platform, they purchase on your behalf in CNY, hold the goods for inspection, and ship internationally. What you pay: product cost + their service fee (usually 5-10%) + international shipping. Still cheaper than Alibaba after all that.

Option B: Use 1688 Directly (For Intermediate Users)

If you have a Chinese payment method (Alipay with international card), you can use 1688 directly. Some Westerners also use a Chinese friend or partner as a buying agent informally.

Finding Reliable Suppliers on 1688

The filtering system on 1688 is powerful. Here is how to use it:

  1. Look for source factory tags -- this means direct manufacturer, not a middleman
  2. Check the Trust Pass badge -- 1688's supplier verification
  3. Filter by location -- Yiwu, Guangzhou, and Hangzhou have the best variety
  4. Read the reviews -- look for high repeat buyer rates
  5. Compare 3-5 suppliers for the same product before deciding

How to Order Samples and Bulk

Always order samples first. No matter how good a listing looks, you must physically inspect the product before committing to a bulk order. On 1688, many suppliers offer one piece dropshipping -- perfect for testing. For bulk orders: message the supplier directly through 1688 chat. Ask for custom logo options, production time, better pricing, and shipping terms.

Getting 1688 Products Shipped to You

Options: through your buying agent (easiest), direct from supplier if you negotiate FOB terms, international couriers like DHL/FedEx/UPS ($30-$80+ per kg), or sea freight for 200kg+ (most economical).

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Skipping samples -- never bulk order without inspecting first
  2. Ignoring MOQ -- some suppliers have 50 or 100 piece minimums
  3. Not calculating landed cost -- product + shipping + customs + last-mile = your real cost
  4. Picking the cheapest price -- extremely low prices usually mean low quality or scams
  5. Not communicating in writing -- always use the platform chat so you have a record

The Bottom Line on 1688

1688 is the single biggest unlock for Western dropshippers. The prices are real, the products are real, and the platform works. It just requires a bit of setup and a buying agent for most people. A product that costs $8 on AliExpress often costs $3-4 on 1688 before shipping. Start with a buying agent, order 3-5 samples from different suppliers, and compare quality. That is how you build a real dropshipping business on China sourcing.
